A **profile** is you — your identity, your AI provider and keys, and the **prompt**
that steers how Patrick works. You can keep several (for example a shared "client
profile" preloaded with that client's style and examples, for consistency).

## What's in a profile

- **Identity** — your name, firm, and **practice context** (a standing description
  of how you work).
- **AI** — your provider (Anthropic / OpenAI / Google), key, models, and reasoning
  effort.
- **Prompt** — the template that instructs Patrick, with tokens like `<TASK>` and
  `<PRACTICECONTEXT>` filled in automatically.
- **Examples** — writing samples so Patrick matches your voice.

## Let Patrick help

The practice context and the prompt are the blank-page parts. Open a task, then
ask Patrick in the chat — it can **draft your practice context or improve your
prompt**, proposed as an accept/reject card.
